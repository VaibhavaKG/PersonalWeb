Projects & Research

Featured Project

From Stardust to Stars: Stellar Evolution and Dynamics

An analytical study of stellar life cycles, examining the physics of molecular cloud collapse, main-sequence nuclear fusion, and the terminal stages of stellar evolution. The study explores the Hertzsprung–Russell diagram, mass–luminosity scaling relations, and compact stellar remnants including white dwarfs, neutron stars, and black holes. It incorporates observational datasets and imagery from NASA, Hubble Space Telescope, and the Event Horizon Telescope to connect theoretical models with modern astrophysical observations.

About

I am a physics major at the Indian Institute of Science Education and Research (IISER) Thiruvananthapuram, working at the intersection of theoretical physics, stellar dynamics, and computational astronomy. My academic journey is driven by a curiosity about the mechanisms that govern extreme environments in the cosmos.

Beyond core physics, I am interested in coding and software development, organizing events as a founding member of the Coding Club and developing web tools for the Science & Tech Council. I also appreciate literature and science writing, exploring how narrative structures can help communicate complex concepts.

Currently focused on undergraduate coursework in quantum mechanics, statistical physics, and data analysis, while compiling observational evidence for stellar structure models.
